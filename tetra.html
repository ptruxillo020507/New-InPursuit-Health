In 18 months, the “best” AI model changed four times. GPT-4 led. Claude surpassed it in reasoning. Gemini moved on multimodal. Llama started winning in specialized domains. Any organization that hardwired to one model is already rearchitecting.

At the clinical level, the divergence is sharper still. One model excels at medication interaction analysis. Another at interpreting wearable ECG data. Another at patient communication in Spanish. No single AI will be best at everything, for every patient, in every moment. That idea is already obsolete.

“Human-in-the-loop is essentially human-on-the-hook, because clinicians find it very difficult to detect AI errors. Any system that relies solely on human oversight is almost certain to fail.”

ARPA-H Program Manager — ADVOCATE Initiative

A supervisory agent built on the same AI model it supervises cannot reliably detect that model’s errors. Same blind spots. Same failure modes. Same architectural vulnerabilities. The supervisor must be structurally independent from the thing it supervises. That is not a preference — it is a safety requirement.

TETRA’s model-agnostic orchestration layer guarantees that independence. It is the infrastructure that makes every clinical AI agent safe, observable, and deployable — regardless of foundation model, regardless of vendor, regardless of what comes next.
