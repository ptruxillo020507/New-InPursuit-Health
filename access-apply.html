Section D
Clinical Care Protocols & Population Health Management Strategy
🔒 Completed by InPursuit · Reviewed by Medical Director
📄
Clinical Protocols — InPursuit Medical Director Authored
These protocols were authored by InPursuit's clinical team under the supervision of our Medical Director and CMS Liaison. They reflect CMS-approved care protocols consistent with AHA/ADA/KDIGO clinical guidelines and are required to meet ACCESS application standards.
Care Coordination Model
AI-augmented, provider-led care coordination using TETRA's real-time clinical decision support. Each enrolled beneficiary is assigned to a care team consisting of the primary provider (quarterback), a care coordinator supported by TETRA automation, and InPursuit's clinical AI layer. Monthly care touchpoints are automatically triggered based on condition severity and OAP requirements.
Early CKM Protocol (HTN / Obesity / Prediabetes)
Structured 90-day intensive lifestyle intervention using validated TETRA patient engagement modules. Blood pressure monitoring via RPM (≥16 days/month per CPT 99454), weight trend tracking, HbA1c trajectory monitoring quarterly. TETRA triggers automated outreach when readings exceed individualized thresholds. Care gap closure tracked against CMS OAP quality metrics in real time.
CKM Full Protocol (DM / CKD / ASCVD)
Intensive chronic disease management with minimum 3–4 physician visits/year enforced via TETRA scheduling automation. HbA1c ≤7% target, eGFR trend monitoring, UACR annual testing, statin therapy adherence tracking. RPM-enabled continuous glucose monitoring integration (Abbott LibreLink, Dexcom) for insulin-dependent patients. Specialist co-management coordination via TEFCA-enabled care summary sharing.
Patient Stratification Methodology
TETRA's AI risk stratification engine assigns each Medicare beneficiary a composite risk score (0–100) updated nightly based on: lab trends (eGFR, HbA1c, BP), medication adherence signals (pharmacy claims via NCPDP), ED/hospital utilization (TEFCA ADT feeds), and social determinant factors (SDOH screening embedded in AWV workflow). High-risk patients (score ≥70) receive proactive outreach within 48 hours of risk score change.
Quality Measure Reporting
✓ TETRA automatically captures, validates, and submits all required ACCESS OAP quality measures to CMS on the required reporting schedule. Supported measures include: BP Control (NQF 0018), DM HbA1c Poor Control (NQF 0059), Statin Use ASCVD (NQF 0438), Depression Screening PHQ-9 (NQF 0418), AWV Completion Rate (CMS Stars), Hospitalization Readmission (CMS 30-day). Provider receives daily compliance dashboard — no manual chart abstraction required.
Clinical Oversight Structure
InPursuit Medical Director reviews all clinical protocol deployments and is available for CMS inquiries. Provider retains full clinical authority — TETRA provides decision support, not autonomous clinical decision-making. All AI-generated recommendations require provider acknowledgment before action.
Section E
Patient Engagement & Beneficiary Experience Strategy
🔒 Completed by InPursuit
📱
Patient Engagement Infrastructure — TETRA Platform
InPursuit provides the complete patient engagement infrastructure required by CMS ACCESS. No patient-facing technology implementation is required from the practice.
Outreach Automation Rate
✓ 70–80% of patient outreach automated via TETRA — SMS, IVR voice call, patient portal message, and secure email. Staff intervention required for ≤30% of contacts. Language preference supported in English, Spanish, and 12 additional languages.
AWV Scheduling & Completion
Automated AWV reminder sequence initiates 90 days before due date: (1) portal message at 90 days, (2) SMS at 60 days with self-scheduling link, (3) IVR voice call at 30 days, (4) staff callback trigger at 14 days if unscheduled. TETRA tracks completion in real time vs. practice baseline and CMS AWV completion benchmark (78%+ target).
Beneficiary Consent Management
✓ TETRA's digital consent module captures and stores ACCESS program consent (CCM, RPM, and all applicable CMS program consents) electronically with full audit trail. Consent stored in patient record and automatically included in CMS compliance documentation.
SDOH Integration
Social Determinants of Health screening embedded within AWV workflow using PRAPARE and AHC-HRSN validated instruments. Results trigger automatic referrals to community resources via InPursuit's 2-1-1 integration layer (transportation, nutrition, housing, medication assistance programs).
Beneficiary Grievance Process
InPursuit maintains a dedicated beneficiary support line (800-InPursuit, option 2) and digital portal for ACCESS program questions. All grievances logged, tracked, and resolved within CMS-required timelines. Practice receives weekly summary report.
