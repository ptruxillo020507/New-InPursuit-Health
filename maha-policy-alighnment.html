Security & Trust

Your Data Is Yours.
We Guard It Like It's Ours.

Every American trusting InPursuit with their health data deserves more than a privacy policy. They deserve infrastructure that was built for the most security-sensitive environments on earth — and an AI oversight system that ensures no algorithm ever makes a decision without a human safety net.

Battle-Tested in Federal Healthcare

TETRA was built for the Department of Defense and Veterans Affairs — environments where a data breach isn't just a fine, it's a national security incident. Our zero-trust security architecture, encryption standards, and access controls meet the highest bar in American healthcare.

Patient-Owned, Patient-Controlled

Your data belongs to you. Period. InPursuit gives every individual full ownership and control of their health records — who sees it, where it goes, and when it's shared. We don't sell data. We don't monetize records. The patient is the customer, not the product.

Complete Audit Trail

Every transaction, every data access, every AI recommendation is logged with microsecond-precision timestamps. 1.8 million transactions per hour — all auditable, all traceable, all accountable. If something happens with your data, we know exactly what, when, and who.

HIPAA, FedRAMP, FHIR/TEFCA Native

Regulatory compliance isn't an afterthought — it's the architecture. TETRA is built natively on FHIR and TEFCA interoperability standards, meets federal security requirements validated through DoD/VA deployment, and maintains less than 1% downtime across all production environments.

ARPA-H ADVOCATE Program

Protecting Americans from AI — Not Just Connecting Them to It

As clinical AI enters healthcare, a critical question remains unanswered: who watches the algorithms? AI systems are making recommendations about medications, care plans, and clinical decisions — but without continuous oversight, errors, hallucinations, and unsafe recommendations go undetected.

You cannot monitor what you cannot observe. You cannot validate decisions without complete clinical context. You cannot ensure safety without reliable real-time data flows. This is fundamentally a data infrastructure problem — and it's exactly what TETRA was built to solve.

InPursuit is developing the supervisory infrastructure platform for the ARPA-H ADVOCATE program — the observability layer that sits between clinical AI agents and patients, ensuring every AI decision is monitored, validated against real clinical data, and flagged the instant it exceeds safe parameters. Not just for cardiovascular disease — for every future clinical AI application.

Department of Health & Human Services

Leading the MAHA Transformation

Under Secretary Robert F. Kennedy Jr., HHS is leading the MAHA transformation with a focus on addressing root causes of chronic disease, reducing pharmaceutical dependency, and empowering Americans to take control of their health through nutrition, lifestyle, and preventive care.

MAHA Commission

Chronic Disease Prevention Coordination

Secretary Kennedy established a MAHA Commission to coordinate chronic disease prevention across all HHS agencies — eliminating ultra-processed foods from federal nutrition programs, reducing environmental toxins, and promoting lifestyle-based interventions.

InPursuit Alignment

TETRA's gamified wellness marketplace directly supports MAHA Commission goals by incentivizing healthy behaviors, tracking nutrition and exercise, and integrating with wearables to provide real-time health feedback.

Revenue Opportunity: $50–100M
FDA — January 2026

Digital Health Deregulation

The FDA announced eased regulation of digital health products, wearables, and AI-enabled devices at CES 2026. Clinical decision support software is now exempt from FDA regulation. Wellness devices have broader leeway for heart rate, blood pressure, and glucose readings.

InPursuit Alignment

This deregulation accelerates InPursuit's ability to deploy AI-powered health recommendations and integrate with consumer wearables without lengthy FDA approval processes.

Impact: Faster Time-to-Market
ARPA-H — $100M+ Program

ADVOCATE Program

ARPA-H launched ADVOCATE (Agentic AI-EnableD CardioVascular CAre TransfOrmation) to develop patient-facing agentic AI for cardiovascular care — addressing 200,000+ preventable deaths annually. Three technical areas: patient-facing AI (TA1), supervisory AI (TA2), and scalable integration (TA3).

InPursuit Alignment

TETRA's AI assistant architecture directly maps to ADVOCATE requirements. Existing conversational AI, wearable integration, and care coordination capabilities position us as a prime contractor or partner.

Revenue Opportunity: $10–50M
Recovery — $100M Investment

Great American Recovery Initiative

A $100M investment for addiction treatment, homelessness, and public safety. Includes the STREETS Initiative, $10M Assisted Outpatient Treatment program, and addition of MOUD as Title IV-E prevention services. Co-chaired by Secretary Kennedy and Senior Advisor Kathryn Burgum.

InPursuit Alignment

TETRA supports addiction recovery through medication adherence tracking, telehealth integration for counseling, and care coordination between treatment providers, social services, and housing programs.

Revenue Opportunity: $5–20M

Centers for Medicare & Medicaid Services

The Engine of Healthcare Transformation

CMS administers healthcare coverage for over 150 million Americans through Medicare and Medicaid. Under the Trump administration, CMS is launching an unprecedented array of technology-enabled, patient-centered initiatives that align perfectly with InPursuit's capabilities.

Cornerstone — July 2025

CMS Health IT Ecosystem

The cornerstone of InPursuit's strategy. CMS created the first Medicare/Medicaid patient-facing app marketplace. InPursuit is the only vendor positioned to deliver all three categories: Kill the Clipboard, Conversational AI Assistants, and Diabetes & Obesity Management.

InPursuit Alignment

TETRA delivers all three app categories through a single integrated platform — a unique market position providing access to 150M+ beneficiaries.

Revenue Opportunity: $30–390M (Year 1–3)
TrumpRX — Feb 2026

Most-Favored-Nation Drug Pricing

TrumpRX.gov launched February 5, 2026 with GoodRx API integration, displaying real-time MFN pricing for 40+ medications. Key pricing includes Ozempic at $350 (vs $1,000 list), Wegovy at $350 (vs $1,350), and Zepbound at $346 (vs $1,086).

InPursuit Alignment

TETRA integrates the TrumpRX API directly into the patient app, displaying real-time MFN pricing and linking to manufacturer portals — a key differentiator in the CMS app marketplace.

Impact: Key Differentiator
ACCESS Model — July 2026

Advancing Chronic Care

A 10-year voluntary model with Outcome-Aligned Payments for tech-enabled chronic care. Covers 67% of Medicare FFS beneficiaries across four clinical tracks: eCKM, CKM, MSK, and Behavioral Health. Applications due April 1, 2026.

InPursuit Alignment

TETRA's chronic disease management capabilities, FHIR-based data exchange, and outcomes tracking directly support ACCESS Model requirements as the technology backbone for participating organizations.

Revenue Opportunity: $50–100M
ELEVATE — September 2026

MAHA ELEVATE Model

The first CMS model focused on proactive, holistic, patient-centered functional/lifestyle medicine. Approximately $100M in 3-year cooperative agreements for up to 30 proposals. Focus: nutrition, physical activity, sleep, stress management, and social connections.

InPursuit Alignment

TETRA's wellness marketplace was built for exactly this — gamified lifestyle interventions, wearable integration, nutrition tracking, and rewards for healthy behaviors.

Revenue Opportunity: $20–50M
BALANCE — May 2026

GLP-1 Lifestyle Support

CMS negotiates GLP-1 drug pricing with manufacturers on behalf of states and Medicare Part D plans. Medicaid launch May 2026; Part D launch January 2027. Includes mandatory lifestyle support programs alongside medication.

InPursuit Alignment

TETRA provides the required lifestyle support programs — nutrition tracking, exercise gamification, and behavioral coaching that accompany GLP-1 prescriptions.

Revenue Opportunity: $10–30M

InPursuit Alignment

TETRA's 5G-enabled telehealth capabilities and rapid deployment (weeks vs quarters) make it ideal for rural health transformation where traditional infrastructure is limited.

Revenue Opportunity: $25–75M

Department of Veterans Affairs

Honoring Those Who Served

The VA serves 9 million veterans through 170 medical centers and over 1,000 outpatient facilities. Under Secretary Doug Collins, the VA is pursuing aggressive modernization with a focus on EHR integration, seamless DoD-VA care transitions, and improved veteran experience.

$4.8B Investment — FY2026

Healthcare Modernization

The largest single-year Non-Recurring Maintenance investment in VA history, including $1 billion specifically for EHR maintenance and modernization.

InPursuit Alignment

TETRA's proven federal deployment capability and EHR integration expertise positions us as a prime vendor for VA modernization initiatives.

Revenue Opportunity: $25–75M
Federal EHR — 170 Sites

EHR Deployment Acceleration

The VA is resuming EHR deployments at 13 sites in 2026, with full deployment to 170 sites by 2031. The Oracle-Cerner system requires significant integration support for patient-facing applications. Lifecycle cost: $37B+.

InPursuit Alignment

TETRA serves as the patient-facing layer on top of the Federal EHR — providing veterans mobile access, scheduling, medication management, and care coordination the Oracle-Cerner system lacks.

Revenue Opportunity: $10–30M per wave

InPursuit Alignment

Department of Defense

Warfighter Readiness & Optimal Human Performance

The Military Health System serves 9.6 million beneficiaries through 46 hospitals, 570 clinics, and the TRICARE program. The FY2026 MHS budget is $64 billion — 7.5% of total DoD spending. When lives depend on split-second decisions, commanders need absolute certainty that their people are operating at peak cognitive and physical readiness.

Warfighter Edge

Optimal Human Performance

Mission success depends on the cognitive and physical readiness of every operator in the field. Sleep quality, stress response, hydration, cognitive load, heart rate variability — these aren't wellness metrics. They're operational intelligence. Commanders need real-time visibility into the readiness of their most valuable assets: their people.

InPursuit Alignment

TETRA integrates with any wearable device — Oura Ring, Garmin, WHOOP, Apple Watch, Polar, tactical-grade biosensors — fusing biometric data streams (HRV, sleep architecture, cognitive performance markers, physical strain) into a single operational readiness dashboard. Unit commanders see real-time force health status. Medics see early warning signs before they become casualties. Decision-makers know their best assets are in the field when it matters most.

Revenue Opportunity: $20–50M
7% Mandate — Dec 2026

Care Reattraction Mandate

DoD is mandated to reattract at least 7% of available care from the private sector back to MTFs by December 31, 2026. Currently, the MHS purchases over 65% of care from civilian providers — driving costs and fragmenting data from the warfighter's health record.

InPursuit Alignment

TETRA helps MTFs compete with civilian providers by offering a superior patient experience — easy scheduling, telehealth access, care coordination, and rewards for engagement. When service members can access better care on-base with a consumer-grade digital experience, they stay in the system.

Revenue Opportunity: $10–30M
MHS GENESIS

Patient Portal + Readiness Layer

MHS GENESIS (DoD's Oracle-Cerner EHR) includes a patient portal, but user satisfaction remains below civilian benchmarks. The system tracks clinical encounters — but not the continuous biometric and performance data that defines operational readiness between visits.

InPursuit Alignment

TETRA serves as an enhanced patient-facing layer integrating with MHS GENESIS, providing the consumer-grade experience beneficiaries expect while adding continuous performance monitoring. Wearable data, cognitive assessments, and readiness scores flow alongside clinical records — giving both the warfighter and the medical system a complete picture.

Revenue Opportunity: $15–40M
